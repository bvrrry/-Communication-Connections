Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about our speech pathology services

If your child is having difficulty being understood, struggling with learning to talk, having trouble following instructions, or facing challenges with reading and writing, speech pathology may help. We offer assessments to identify your child's specific needs and develop a tailored therapy plan.
We provide services for children from birth through to 18 years of age. Our therapy approaches are tailored to each developmental stage, from early intervention for babies and toddlers through to support for teenagers preparing for adulthood.
Yes, we are registered NDIS providers and can work with families who have NDIS plans. We can provide support with plan management and help you understand how to access speech pathology services through your NDIS funding.
Standard therapy sessions are typically 45-60 minutes, depending on your child's age, needs, and attention span. For younger children or those with specific needs, we may recommend shorter, more frequent sessions.
Absolutely! We encourage family involvement in therapy. Parent and caregiver participation is an important part of our approach, as it helps ensure strategies are carried over into daily life and supports your child's progress between sessions.
Every child is different, and the number of sessions depends on their individual needs and goals. After an initial assessment, we'll discuss a therapy plan with you and provide recommendations for frequency and duration. We regularly review progress and adjust the plan as needed.
